Manifesto

ZALTURI is not a brand. It's a frequency you can tune into — and, if you want, broadcast on.

No face. No names.

A few independent musicians, no logo to protect, no image to sell. We took the face off on purpose. What's left is the sound and the joke — just signal in the dark. The mask isn't mystery for mystery's sake; it's so the work doesn't get reduced to who made it.

What we make

Bootleg edits and remixes of whatever is trending, stitched together with original experiments. We take the songs the algorithm shoves at everyone and bend them — sometimes lovingly, sometimes cruelly, usually both.

It runs on irony, post-irony and meta-irony, and on the freedom to not tell you which layer you're standing on. If a track is a joke about trends, a tribute to them, and a middle finger to them at once — good. That's the point.

  • edits & bootlegs of trending tracks
  • remixes that take the joke too far on purpose
  • originals, when the originals want out

Why it's free

Because it was never meant to be a product. There's no monetization here, by design — no ads, no paywall, no "support us," no algorithm rationing the music out to you a little at a time. You press play or you take the files. That's the whole transaction.

Call it an exit door from the streaming machine. The corporate music economy turns everything into engagement; ZALTURI turns it back into an outlet — for the people making it and the people hearing it.

About the rights

Let's be honest: these are edits. The rights aren't cleared, because clearing rights is for products, and this isn't one. Everything is free, non-commercial, and transformative — made as commentary and shared as a gift, never for sale.

If you hold rights to something here and you'd rather it wasn't, say the word and it's gone. No fight, no profit, nothing to defend. We're not here to take anything from anyone — we're here to make noise and give it away.

Anyone can be ZALTURI

This is the part that matters. ZALTURI is less a band than a way of releasing. The name is open. Make something, mean it, share it free, put on the mask. If you do that, you're ZALTURI too.
